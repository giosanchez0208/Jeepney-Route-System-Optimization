2. Redundancy Repulsion (Refining my second idea) Moving a highly useful segment away from a low-served area provides no mathematical benefit. 
If a segment is highly useful, it is exactly where passengers need it to be. Moving it breaks existing transit flows.

I can isolate one of the overlapping routes and "repel" its segment to a parallel street.
Mechanism: This maintains the general directional utility of the transit corridor while expanding spatial coverage to adjacent blocks.
Academic Justification: I refer to this as Dispersion Routing or Route Overlap Minimization.

3. Transit Tortuosity Pruning (A proposed third strategy) Urban transit passengers penalize circuitous routes. 
I identify low-pheromone segments that meander between two high-demand hubs. I delete the useless detour entirely. 
I bridge the resulting gap using the strictest shortest-path calculation available on the city graph.
Mechanism: This straightens the route. It converts wasted fleet travel time into higher frequency service between profitable hubs.
Academic Justification: I refer to this as Tortuosity Reduction or Route Directness Optimization.
